# Scenario Playbooks

Use these as compact defaults. Adjust to user profile, weather, wardrobe, and intent.

## Business Meeting

Goal: reliable, prepared, respectful, not distracting.

- Prefer clean lines, controlled color, polished shoes, structured or semi-structured outer layer.
- If the user dislikes stiff materials, use knit blazer, soft jacket, cardigan-jacket, crisp shirt, tailored trousers, loafers.
- Avoid: overly casual sneakers, distressed denim, loud logos, high-maintenance pieces.

## Interview

Goal: competent, clear, role-fit, comfortable under pressure.

- Match industry formality. Traditional industries need more structure; creative roles can include one personal note.
- Keep colors calm and face-brightening.
- Avoid new shoes or fussy pieces.

## Commuting / Workday

Goal: efficient, comfortable, repeatable, weather-aware.

- Prioritize mobility, layers, shoes, bag practicality.
- Make one detail intentional: belt, scarf, earrings, watch, bag color, shoe shape.

## Weekend Friends / Cafe / Exhibition

Goal: relaxed, clean, personal, not overdone.

- Use softer layers, denim/chinos/skirt, comfortable shoes, one texture or color note.
- Avoid looking like office wear unless intentionally softened.

## Date / Social Dinner

Goal: approachable, comfortable, considered.

- Calibrate boundary and intimacy through fabric, neckline, fit, and accessories.
- Add one tactile or light-catching element.
- Avoid anything that requires constant adjusting.

## Weather Rules

- Rain: avoid floor-grazing hems, suede, pale absorbent shoes, fragile bags.
- Wind: avoid uncontrolled light skirts without layering or weights.
- Heat: prioritize breathable fabric, looser fit, lighter color, sweat-safe layers.
- Cold: preserve silhouette with thin warm layers, not only bulky outerwear.
